FESTIVALS TO ATTEND

Dashain

October 15th- 28th

Dashain, also referred as Vijaya Dashami in Sanskrit, is a major Hindu religious festival in Nepal. It is the longest and the most auspicious festival, celebrated by Nepali Hindus, along with their diaspora throughout the globe. In Nepal, it is also known as the biggest festival in the country and is the longest national/public holiday, lasting 15 days.

Tihar

November 11th- 15th

Tihar also known as Deepawali is a five-day Hindu festival celebrated in Nepal. Nepal's various communities celebrate Tihar in different ways. The festival is popularly known as Swanti among the Newars and as Deepawali among Madhesis.

Indra Jatra

September 17th, 18thand 21st

Indra Jatra, also known as Yenya Punhi is the biggest religious street festival in Kathmandu, Nepal. Indra Jatra is marked by masked dances of deities and demons, displays of sacred images, and tableaus in honor of the deity Indra, the king of heaven. The main venue of the festivities isKathmandu Durbar Square and lasts for eight days

Holi

March 24th

Holi is a popular and significant Hindu festival celebrated as the Festival of Colours, Love, and Spring. It is a cultural celebration that gives Hindus and non-Hindus alike an opportunity to have fun by throwing coloured water and powder at each other. It lasts for a night and a day, starting on the evening of the Purnima (full moon day).
