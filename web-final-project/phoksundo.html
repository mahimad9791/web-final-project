SHEY PHOKSUNDO

Shey Phoksundo Lake

Shey Phoksundo lake trek is an incredible trail in the Dolpa district of Nepal. Phoksundo lake is a fresh alpine water oligotrophic lake and is popular for its amazing views, natural settlement, and mind-blowing surroundings. Shey Phoksundo lake is situated at an altitude of 3611 m above sea level. It also carries a huge religious significance. Many people from Buddhism and the Bon community worship at Phoksundo Lake. According to religious belief, it is said that there lies an underwater world in Phoksundo lake.

Phoksundo lake trek got its popularity after a famous writer Peter Matthiessen wrote the book ``The Snow Leopard”. The alluring beauty of Shey Phoksundo lake and the myths and beliefs are vividly composed in his book. The astonishing and soothing atmosphere of the Dolpo religion is not so explored but offers a wonderful spectrum of the mountain lifestyle, cultural diversity, and biological variations. Trekking in the Lower Dolpo and Shey Phoksundo area offers a pristine and natural adventurous advantage while you get to explore the most beautiful landscape in the mountains of Nepal.



How to reach Phoksundo lake?

Phoksundo lake is an amazing place in the trans mountain region of Nepal. The only way to get there is via trekking and it offers a wide range of experiences throughout your journey. A government-registered guide and porter will assist you to complete your adventure. The trekking to Phoksundo lake begins with a flight from Kathmandu to Nepalgunj. Another flight from Nepalgunj will take you to Juphal, which is also the trek starting point of Phoksundo Lake Trek.

Exploring the astonishing atmosphere and off the beaten paths, moving through the villages such as Dunai, Chhepka, Jharana, Ringmo villages, you will reach Shey Phoksundo. Most of the parts in this trekking area lie in the Shey Phoksundo National Park territory. And the returning trail also follows the villages Ringmo, Chhepka, Dunai and finally takes you back to Juphal. A flight from Jupal will take you back to Nepalgunj and another connecting flight will take you back to the Kathmandu valley.


The best time to visit Shey Phoksundo Lake

It is generally considered to be from May to October. Here’s a breakdown of the different seasons during this period:


Spring (May to mid-June):

Spring is a beautiful time to visit Shey Phoksundo Lake as the region comes alive with blooming flowers, lush greenery, and cascading waterfalls. The temperatures are mild, ranging from around 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F) during the day, making it comfortable for trekking. However, it’s worth noting that the higher altitudes may still be chilly, especially during the nights. The skies are generally clear, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.


Summer (mid-June to August):

The summer months bring the monsoon season to Nepal, including the Dolpa region. While other popular trekking regions in Nepal experience heavy rainfall during this time, Dolpa lies in a rain-shadow area, meaning it receives less precipitation. Trekking to Shey Phoksundo Lake during the monsoon season is possible, but it’s important to be prepared for occasional rainfall and the possibility of muddy trails. The temperatures range from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F), and the landscapes are lush and vibrant.


Autumn (September to October):

Autumn is considered the best season for trekking in Nepal, including the Shey Phoksundo Lake region. The weather during this time is generally stable, with clear skies, mild temperatures, and low chances of rainfall. The days are pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F), while the nights can be cooler, especially at higher altitudes. The autumn months offer excellent visibility, allowing trekkers to enjoy panoramic views of the Himalayas and the pristine lake.

Difficulty

The trekking is comparatively difficult as it is an off the beaten path trek. However, the trails are well defined, making it easier to trek in the lower Dolpo area. There is a chance of Acute mountain sickness, however, it's situated at an altitude of 3611 m above sea level, which generally does not take acclimatization left to adapt. You will be gaining 500-600 m altitude every day on average, which is not so difficult.

In terms of health, trekkers should be fit enough to climb uphill mountains. Also, there is less accessibility in the Dolpo area, so getting prepared for the uncertainty is a good choice.

In conclusion, a trek to Shey Phoksundo Lake is a remarkable and transformative experience. The pristine beauty of the lake, nestled amidst the Himalayan mountains, provides a breathtaking backdrop for adventurers and nature enthusiasts. The trek offers a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural encounters, and spiritual connections with the surrounding landscape.

From the moment you arrive in Kathmandu and make your way to Shey Phoksundo Lake, you will be captivated by the stunning landscapes, the traditional villages, and the warm hospitality of the local people. The trek allows you to witness the unique geological features of the region, including the trans-Himalayan landscapes, deep gorges, and high mountain passes.
